Step by Step Guide to Yoga at Home



Step 1


Mountain Pose (Tadasana):

Stand at the front of your mat with your feet hip-width apart and arms by your sides.

Ground your feet firmly into the mat, engage your core, and relax your shoulders.

Close your eyes and take deep, slow breaths for 1-2 minutes, focusing on your posture and the present moment.


Step 2


Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana):

From Mountain Pose, bend forward at the hips, placing your palms on the mat shoulder-width apart.

Step your feet back, creating an inverted "V" shape with your body.

Press your palms into the mat, engage your core, and lengthen your spine.

Hold this pose for 3-5 breaths, pedaling your feet to stretch your calves and hamstrings.


Step 3


Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II):

From Downward-Facing Dog, step your right foot forward between your hands and rotate your left foot 90 degrees to the left.

Bend your right knee to a 90-degree angle, aligning it with your ankle.

Stretch your arms out parallel to the floor, with your gaze over your right fingertips.

Hold for 3-5 breaths, feeling the strength and stability in your legs.


Step 4


Tree Pose (Vrksasana):

Stand up straight and shift your weight onto your left foot.

Bend your right knee and place the sole of your right foot on the inside of your left thigh, just above the knee.

Bring your hands together in a prayer position at your heart center.

Focus on a point in front of you to help with balance.

Hold for 3-5 breaths, then switch to the other leg.


Step 5


Child's Pose (Balasana):

Kneel on the mat with your big toes touching and knees apart.

Sit back on your heels and reach your arms forward, lowering your forehead to the mat.

Relax in this resting pose for 3-5 deep breaths, allowing your spine to lengthen and your hips to relax.


Step 6


Corpse Pose (Savasana):

Lie flat on your back with your legs and arms extended comfortably.

Close your eyes and let go of all tension in your body.

Focus on your breath and allow yourself to completely relax for at least 5-10 minutes, letting go of any thoughts.





Step by Step Guide to Pilates at Home


Step 1


Pelvic Curl:

Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet hip-width apart, and arms by your sides.

Inhale, and as you exhale, tilt your pelvis, curling your spine off the mat one vertebra at a time.

Lift your hips until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees.

Inhale at the top and exhale as you lower your spine back down to the mat, one vertebra at a time.

Perform 8-10 repetitions.


Step 2


Hundred:

Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet flat on the mat, and arms at your sides.

Lift your head, neck, and shoulders off the mat, engaging your core.

Pump your arms up and down vigorously as you breathe in for five counts and out for five counts.

Keep your legs in tabletop position (knees bent at a 90-degree angle) or extend them straight up for more challenge.

Repeat for 10 breath cycles (100 arm pumps).


Step 3


Single Leg Stretch:

Lie on your back with your knees pulled into your chest and your head and shoulders lifted off the mat.

Extend your right leg straight while holding your left knee into your chest.

Switch legs by bringing your left knee in as you extend your right leg.

Continue alternating legs while keeping your core engaged.

Perform 10-12 repetitions on each leg.


Step 4


Plank:

Come into a push-up position with your hands directly under your shoulders.

Engage your core and maintain a straight line from head to heels.

Hold this position for 20-30 seconds, gradually working up to longer durations as you build strength.


Step 5


Pilates Swimming:

Lie face down on the mat with your arms extended overhead and legs straight.

Lift your arms, head, chest, and legs slightly off the mat.

Begin alternating lifting your right arm and left leg and then your left arm and right leg while simultaneously flutter-kicking your legs.

Inhale and exhale rhythmically as you perform this exercise for 30 seconds to 1 minute.


Step 6


Spine Stretch Forward:

Sit up tall with your legs extended straight in front of you, feet flexed.

Inhale to lengthen your spine, and as you exhale, hinge at your hips to reach your hands toward your feet.

Keep your back straight, and feel the stretch in your hamstrings.

Inhale to sit back up tall, and repeat for 4-6 repetitions.





Step by Step Guide to Medatating at Home


Step 1

Find a Quiet Space:

Choose a quiet and comfortable place where you won't be disturbed. You can sit on a cushion, chair, or even lie down if that's more comfortable for you.


Step 2

Set a Timer:

Decide on the duration of your meditation session. Beginners might start with 5-10 minutes and gradually increase the time as they become more experienced. Set a timer to signal the end of your meditation.


Step 3

Sit Comfortably:

Find a comfortable posture that allows you to sit with a straight back. You can sit cross-legged on the floor or in a chair with your feet flat on the ground. Keep your hands resting on your lap or your knees.


Step 4

Focus on Your Breath:

Close your eyes and begin to focus on your breath. Pay attention to the sensation of the breath as it enters and leaves your nostrils or the rise and fall of your chest or abdomen.

If your mind starts to wander, gently bring your attention back to your breath without judgment. The goal is not to eliminate thoughts but to observe them and let them pass.


Step 5

Body Scan:

After a few minutes of focused breathing, shift your attention to your body. Start from the top of your head and slowly move your awareness down through each part of your body.

Notice any areas of tension or discomfort and allow them to relax as you continue to breathe deeply.


Step 6

Gratitude or Positive Affirmation:

To conclude your meditation, bring your attention back to your breath for a few moments. Then, think of something you're grateful for or a positive affirmation that resonates with you.

Hold this thought in your mind and heart for a moment, feeling the gratitude or positivity wash over you.

Optional Step: You can end your meditation session by gently opening your eyes and taking a few deep breaths before resuming your daily activities.
